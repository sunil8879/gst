Economic Order Quantity

Inventory Efficiency AI

Optimal Order Size
707
Units Per Order
Ordering this amount minimizes your total costs for ordering and holding inventory.

The Variables of Wealth

D: Annual Demand

This represents the total quantity of a product your business plans to sell or use over a 12-month period. Accuracy is key to avoiding overstock.

S: Ordering Cost

The fixed cost incurred every time you place an order. Includes shipping, handling, administrative work, and quality inspection fees.

H: Holding Cost

The cost of "carrying" stock in your warehouse. Includes rent, insurance, security, and the risk of the product becoming obsolete or damaged.

EOQ = √ (2DS / H)

Practical Application

Using the default inputs: Demand of 10,000 units, Order Cost of ₹500, and Holding Cost of ₹20:

1. Multiply 2 × 10,000 × 500 = 10,000,000

2. Divide by 20 = 500,000

3. Square Root of 500,000 = 707.10

Result: 707 Units is your Economic Order Quantity.

Strategic Impact Analysis

If EOQ is too LOW

Ordering too little too often creates "Order Fatigue":

  • Skyrocketing administrative and shipping costs.
  • Increased risk of stockouts and lost customers.
  • Inability to leverage bulk purchase discounts.

If EOQ is too HIGH

Ordering too much at once creates "Dead Inventory":

  • Massive amounts of capital tied up in stock.
  • Increased warehouse costs (rent/cooling/insurance).
  • High risk of spoilage, damage, or fashion/tech changes.
