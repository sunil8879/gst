THE FORMULA: (Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

Numerical Example

If your daily sales are 20 units, and your supplier takes 5 days to deliver, you will sell 100 units while waiting for the truck. If you keep 50 units as an emergency buffer (Safety Stock):

ROP = (20 × 5) + 50
ROP = 100 + 50 = 150 Units

Glossary of Terms

Average Daily Demand

The consistent number of products you sell every 24 hours. Knowing this prevents you from underestimating how fast your shelves empty.

Lead Time

The "waiting period." It’s the total time from when you click 'Order' to when the items are actually sitting in your warehouse ready to sell.

Safety Stock

Your insurance policy. These are extra units kept in the back room to handle unexpected spikes in demand or transport delays.

The Risks of Incorrect ROP

If ROP is too LOW

  • Stockouts: You run out of products before the new shipment arrives.
  • Lost Sales: Customers go to competitors when they see "Out of Stock."
  • Backlog Stress: High pressure on staff to manage angry customer queries.

If ROP is too HIGH

  • Tied Capital: Your money is sitting on shelves instead of being invested elsewhere.
  • Holding Costs: Higher electricity, rent, and insurance for a crowded warehouse.
  • Obsolescence: Risk of items expiring or becoming outdated before they sell.
