8. MODEL TENANCY ACT (MTA) APPLICABILITY

This Agreement shall be governed by the applicable tenancy laws in force in the State/Union Territory where the property is situated.

In the event that the said State/Union Territory has adopted or implemented provisions of the Model Tenancy Act, 2021, the parties agree that the tenancy shall be subject to such provisions to the extent applicable, including but not limited to execution of a written tenancy agreement, regulation of security deposit, and dispute resolution through the prescribed Rent Authority or competent court.

In jurisdictions where the Model Tenancy Act has not been adopted, this Agreement shall be governed by the existing Rent Control laws and the provisions of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

9. STAMP DUTY & REGISTRATION

This Agreement is executed on a non-judicial stamp paper or e-stamp of appropriate value as per the applicable Stamp Act of the State in which the property is situated. The parties hereby declare that the stamp duty has been duly paid in accordance with the prevailing laws.

The cost of stamp duty and registration charges, if applicable, shall be borne by the Landlord, unless otherwise mutually agreed between the parties.

This Agreement shall be registered, if required, under the provisions of the Registration Act, 1908. In case the term of this Agreement exceeds 11 months, or where state laws mandate compulsory registration, the parties agree to complete the registration within the prescribed time and shall cooperate by providing all necessary documents.

E-Stamp Declaration: This Agreement may be executed on e-stamp paper generated through an authorized government portal (such as SHCIL or the respective State portal). The unique e-stamp certificate number, date of generation, and QR code shall be considered valid proof of stamp duty payment under applicable laws.
