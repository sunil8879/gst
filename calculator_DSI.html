Average time taken to convert stock into sales.

Key Terminology

Average Inventory

The average value of stock held during a specific timeframe. It accounts for fluctuations in stock levels between the start and end of the period.

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

The direct costs attributed to the production or purchase of the goods sold by a company. It is the baseline cost before adding profit margins.

Measurement Period

Usually calculated over a 365-day fiscal year to show the annual efficiency of inventory management.

Numerical Case Study

Formula: (Average Inventory / COGS) × 365

Consider a business with the following annual figures:

  • Average Inventory: ₹2,00,000
  • Cost of Goods Sold: ₹8,00,000
(2,00,000 / 8,00,000) × 365
0.25 × 365 = 91.25 Days

✅ Interpretation: This company takes roughly 91 days to sell its entire inventory. This means they cycle their stock about 4 times a year.

Strategic Impact Analysis

If DSI is LOW (Fast)

A lower DSI indicates high efficiency and fast-moving stock:

  • Lean Operations: Cash is not tied up in stock for long.
  • Reduced Costs: Lower warehouse rent, insurance, and security costs.
  • Freshness: Lower risk of stock becoming obsolete or expired.

If DSI is HIGH (Slow)

A higher DSI suggests sluggish sales or overstocking:

  • Stagnant Capital: Your money is sitting on shelves, not generating growth.
  • Holding Costs: High expense for maintaining unsold inventory.
  • Market Risk: High risk that items will become outdated before they sell.
