Standard Bank Formula

E = P × R × [ (1 + R)^N / ((1 + R)^N - 1) ]

Where:
R = Annual Rate / (12 × 100)
P = Principal Amount
N = Tenure in Months
Step-by-Step Example (₹10 Lakhs @ 10.5% for 5 Yrs):
R = 10.5 / 1200 = 0.00875
EMI = 1,000,000 × 0.00875 × [ (1+0.00875)^60 / ((1+0.00875)^60 - 1) ]
Result: ₹21,494 / Month
Bank Method: Reducing Balance Example

Why is the EMI always the same? Although you pay ₹21,494 every month, the bank changes the "internal split" of your money. In the early months, the bank takes more Interest. As your loan balance reduces, they take more Principal. This standard formula ensures your monthly budget remains fixed while your debt gradually vanishes.

1. Reducing Balance Method

Used by: SBI, HDFC, ICICI (Standard)

Interest is calculated only on the remaining principal. As you pay, the interest component drops.

Cheaper for User

2. Flat Rate Method

Formula: (P + (P * n * r)) / n

Interest is calculated on the full initial loan amount for the entire duration. It never reduces.

Example: ₹10L @ 10.5% Flat = ₹25,417 EMI (Expensive!)

Note: Monthly Interest is calculated as: (Opening Balance × Rate) / 12.
Principal Paid is simply (Fixed EMI minus Monthly Interest).

Understanding the Figures:

  • Opening Balance: The amount you still owe the bank at the start of that month.
  • Interest (A): The "Profit" the bank takes this month. (Calculated as: Current Balance × Monthly Rate).
  • Principal (B): The "Real" debt reduction. This part of your EMI actually reduces your loan.
  • Closing Balance: Opening Balance minus Principal (B). This becomes next month's Opening Balance.
