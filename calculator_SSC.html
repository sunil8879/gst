Defining the Parameters

Maximum Daily Usage

The absolute peak number of units sold during your busiest day (e.g., during a holiday or sale event).

Maximum Lead Time

The "Worst Case Scenario" for delivery. The longest time your supplier has ever taken to restock you.

Average Daily Usage

Your typical, every-day sales volume under normal operating conditions.

Average Lead Time

The standard time it takes for an order to arrive from your supplier under normal conditions.

Numerical Case Study

Assume your store experiences a sudden rush and logistics delays:

Max Usage (40) × Max Lead (8) = 320
Avg Usage (20) × Avg Lead (5) = 100

Safety Stock = 320 - 100 = 220 Units

✅ Action: You should always have 220 units set aside. This "buffer" ensures that even if sales hit the roof and the truck is late, your shelves stay full.

Impact Analysis

If Safety Stock is LOW

  • Stockout Risk: A single shipping delay could leave you with empty shelves.
  • Customer Churn: Customers will switch to competitors if items are unavailable.
  • Panic Ordering: You may pay high "rush fees" to restock quickly.

If Safety Stock is HIGH

  • Tied Capital: Your cash is "rotting" on shelves instead of being invested elsewhere.
  • Holding Costs: You pay extra for warehouse space, insurance, and security.
  • Waste Risk: Items may expire or become obsolete before they are ever sold.
