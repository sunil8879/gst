Key Terminology

Fixed Costs

These are "Life Support" costs. They stay the same even if you sell nothing. Examples: Office rent, permanent staff salaries, insurance, and equipment leases.

Selling Price Per Unit

The total amount of money your customer pays for exactly one item. This is the top-line revenue generated per transaction.

Variable Cost Per Unit

Costs that "breathe" with your sales. If you produce more, these go up. Examples: Raw materials, packaging, delivery fees, and sales commissions.

Numerical Case Study

Formula: Fixed Costs ÷ (Price - Variable Cost)

Let's calculate based on a startup business:

  • Fixed Costs: ₹50,000
  • Selling Price: ₹200
  • Variable Cost: ₹120
50,000 / (200 - 120)
50,000 / 80 = 625

✅ Analysis: You need to sell 625 units just to pay your bills. Every unit sold after 625 adds directly to your profit!

Impact Analysis

If BEP is LOW

  • Safety: You can survive market downturns much easier.
  • Speed: You start making profit very early in the month.
  • Flexibility: You have room to lower prices to fight competitors.

If BEP is HIGH

  • Risk: A small dip in sales could lead to a massive financial loss.
  • Pressure: Your sales team needs to maintain high volume constantly.
  • Urgency: You should look for ways to cut fixed costs (like cheaper rent) or increase price.
